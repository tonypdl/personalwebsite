🍊 Policy #1: Cut the State Sales Tax in Half

What It Does:

Lowers the state sales tax from 6.25% to 3.125%

Why It Matters:

Sales tax is the most regressive tax in Texas. Everyone pays the same rate, whether you're wealthy or working-class. Cutting it in half means immediate relief for every Texan, every time they make a purchase.

Estimated Annual Cost:

$15.5 billion

Impact:

  • Average family saves over $625 a year — no forms, no delays
  • Boosts local economies by keeping more money in people's pockets

Message:

"A real tax cut that reaches the checkout line. Less taken from you, every single day."
🪜 Policy #2: End the Sales Tax on Essentials

What It Does:

Exempts the following from sales tax:

  • Groceries
  • Diapers
  • Period products
  • School supplies
  • Clothing under $80
  • Texas-made goods

Why It Matters:

Essentials shouldn't be taxed. Working families pay the most under current law. This change directly lowers the cost of raising kids, going to school, and getting by.

Estimated Annual Cost:

$3.0 billion

Impact:

  • Bigger savings for low- and middle-income Texans
  • Helps parents, students, and seniors on fixed incomes

Message:

"No one should be taxed for buying what they need to live."
🎉 Policy #3: Three Week-Long Sales Tax Holidays

What It Does:

Adds three new week-long sales tax holidays:

  • August: Back-to-School Week
  • January: Winter Essentials + Small Business Weekend
  • April: Storm Prep Week

Why It Matters:

The current tax-free weekends are short and poorly timed. A full week gives working families time to plan and shop when it counts most.

Estimated Annual Cost:

$400 million

Impact:

  • Predictable relief when people need it
  • Encourages shopping at Texas businesses during key times of year

Message:

"Three weeks a year where your money goes further — when it matters most."

💵 Policy #20: End Dark Money & Corporate PAC Cash

Policy Title: Clean Up Texas Politics

What It Does:

  • Bans undisclosed "dark money" from influencing Texas elections
  • Prohibits candidates from taking corporate PAC money, ensuring they answer to voters—not billionaires

How It Works:

  • Require full disclosure of donors to nonprofits spending in state-level elections
  • Cap or ban corporate and lobbyist PAC donations directly to campaigns
  • Toughen enforcement of ethics rules and penalties

Why It Matters:

  • Texas saw over $150 million in dark-money funding in recent cycles, with wealthy special interests buying access and agenda control
  • Politicians are too often voting for donors' interests, not community needs
  • Texas consistently fails to pass meaningful ethics reforms—voters deserve leaders who won't be puppets

Impact:

  • Restores trust: voters will know who's funding campaigns
  • Shifts power away from backroom billionaire influence to everyday Texans
  • Reduces pressure on legislators to favor corporate interests over public interest

Message:

"Texas elections shouldn't be for sale. We're shutting the money spigots for billionaires—and giving the power back to people."
