What It Does:

  • This plan gives everyone a fair shot and puts people back in charge

🍊 Policy #1: Cut the State Sales Tax in Half

What It Does:

Lowers the state sales tax from 6.25% to 3.125%.

Why It Matters:

Sales tax hits working Texans the hardest. Everyone pays the same rate, no matter their income. Cutting it in half means you keep more of your money every time you shop.

Estimated Annual Cost:

$15.5 billion

Impact:

  • The average family saves over $625 a year. No paperwork or waiting required.
  • Local businesses benefit as people have more to spend in their communities.

Message:

"This is a real tax cut you feel every time you check out. You keep more of what you earn."
🪜 Policy #2: End the Sales Tax on Essentials

What It Does:

Removes sales tax from:

  • Groceries
  • Diapers
  • Period products
  • School supplies
  • Clothing under $80
  • Texas-made goods

Why It Matters:

Basic needs should not be taxed. This change helps families, students, and seniors by lowering the cost of everyday living.

Estimated Annual Cost:

$3.0 billion

Impact:

  • More savings for low- and middle-income Texans
  • Helps parents, students, and those on fixed incomes

Message:

"You shouldn't pay extra for what you need to get by."
🎉 Policy #3: Three Week-Long Sales Tax Holidays

What It Does:

Creates three new week-long sales tax holidays:

  • August: Back-to-School Week
  • January: Winter Essentials and Small Business Weekend
  • April: Storm Prep Week

Why It Matters:

Current tax-free weekends are too short and not well-timed. A full week gives families time to plan and shop when it matters most.

Estimated Annual Cost:

$400 million

Impact:

  • Gives relief when people need it most
  • Encourages shopping at Texas businesses during important times of year

Message:

"Three weeks a year when your money stretches further, right when you need it."

🌿 Policy #6: Medical Marijuana & Delta Regulation

Policy Title: Regulate and Tax Medical Cannabis & THC Products

What It Does:

  • Legalizes and regulates medical marijuana in Texas
  • Adds licensing, testing, and excise tax to Delta-8, Delta-10, and other THC products

Why It Matters:

Cannabis is already being used in Texas. We should regulate it for safety and tax it to help fund relief. A medical-first approach avoids full recreational legalization but still brings in meaningful revenue.

Estimated Annual Revenue:

$1.0 billion

Message:

"Let's regulate what's already here and use it to cut taxes and invest in Texans."

Guarantees 14 days of early voting across Texas.

Message:

💵 Policy #20: End Dark Money & Corporate PAC Cash

Policy Title: Clean Up Texas Politics

What It Does:

  • Bans undisclosed "dark money" from Texas elections
  • Prohibits candidates from taking corporate PAC money, so they answer to voters, not billionaires

How It Works:

  • Requires full disclosure of donors to nonprofits spending in state-level elections
  • Caps or bans corporate and lobbyist PAC donations directly to campaigns
  • Toughens enforcement of ethics rules and penalties

Why It Matters:

  • Texas has seen over $150 million in dark-money funding in recent cycles, letting wealthy interests buy influence
  • Politicians often vote for donors, not communities
  • Texas needs real ethics reforms so voters can trust their leaders

Impact:

  • Restores trust by letting voters see who funds campaigns
  • Shifts power away from billionaires and back to Texans
  • Reduces pressure on lawmakers to favor corporate interests over the public

Message:

"Texas elections shouldn't be for sale. We're closing the money spigot for billionaires and giving the power back to people."
