The Fair Share Texas Tax Reform

End Socialism for Corporations. Help Working Texans Now. Fund Our Future.

Texans are getting hammered by rising costs while massive corporations write themselves loopholes and skip the bill.
This “Fair Share Tax Reform” plan delivers direct relief to working families with sales tax exemptions, new tax free weeks, and ends corporate tax breaks while working Texans and small businesses have borne the brunt of the post-Covid inflation economy. We make new revenue and reinvest that money immediately in the systems that keep our state running.

What This Plan Delivers

  • Ends the sales tax on basic clothing — no sales tax on any clothing under $100
  • Creates three new week-long sales tax holidays:
    • August: Back-to-school week
    • January: Winter essentials + small business weekend
    • April: Storm prep supplies
  • This would cost about $2B on the high end estimate. Helps working Texans where they need it most: their pockets.
  • We gain revenue to pay for this and more by making corporations pay their fair share, legalizing medical marijuana (like Florida), and regulating online fantasy betting.

Make Corporations Pay Their Fair Share

While Texans have faced hard times post-Covid, renters and homeowners alike, corporations have boomed while wages and support stagnated. Time for corporations to pitch in to help make Texas the place it can be and aid working Texans’ communities and pockets.

Protect Texan Workers Plan

A New Vision for Working Texans

If you work in Texas, you shouldn’t have to live in poverty. You deserve power, protection, and a path forward. This is for all employees: salaried workers, wage workers, gig workers, and everyone in between.

Non-Negotiable “Texan Worker” Bill of Rights

  • Raise the minimum wage to $15
  • Minimum seven days paid sick leave
  • Minimum two weeks of paid time off
  • Humane heat protection laws (breaks, shift length, water, etc.)
  • Ban employer retaliation against employees for whistleblowing, organizing, or speaking out against working conditions or unfair/discriminatory practices
  • Allow for organizing and petitions among employees
  • End the ban on public sector “meet-and-confer” across all districts, cities, and agencies
  • Allow “non-binding collective action” rights for teachers, librarians, and state/public employees

Corporate Accountability for Public Subsidies

  • Require corporations receiving public subsidies or tax breaks to:
    • Disclose wage data by race, gender, and job class
    • Report safety violations and resolution timelines
    • Submit a basic “worker dignity plan” as part of the deal

Companies in Texas will treat Texans as the best workers that they are.

"Texans don’t want a handout. They want a fair shot and the freedom to speak up, team up, and demand better."

THE FAIR BALLOT PLAN

Let Texans Choose. Let New Voices Rise.

Texas has one of the most exclusive and outdated ballot access systems in the country, and that’s by design. The rules are written to protect incumbents, shut out independent voices, and make sure voters never see more than two names on a ballot.
The Fair Ballot Plan fixes that. It opens up the process, gives voters more real choices, and makes democracy work for the people again, not the political gatekeepers.

What This Plan Does

Open Up the Ballot: Real Choice for Texas Voters

Texas has some of the harshest ballot access laws in the country, designed to shut out independent and third-party voices before voters even get a chance to hear them. We change that by making the rules fair, modern, and open:

  • Drastically reduce signature requirements for independent and minor party candidates
  • Lower filing fees and allow as few as 100 voter signatures, not 500 or more, to qualify
  • Let all registered voters sign petitions, not just those who skipped primaries
  • Allow online petition signing for independent and third-party candidates
  • Extend deadlines to give candidates real time to collect signatures
  • Set signature thresholds at 0.1% of the statewide vote; about 8,000, not 80,000
  • End the “one-and-done” rule that bars parties from trying again after a single setback
  • Create alternate access paths for new parties, including:
    • 10,000 verified dues-paying members
    • Conventions with at least 1,000 attendees
    • Automatic ballot access for any party that earns 2%+ in a recent statewide race

This isn’t about helping any party, it’s about respecting voters. Texans deserve more than two choices. Let the people decide who earns their support.

Legalize Fusion Voting

Allow a candidate to appear on the ballot under more than one party. That means a candidate could be endorsed by both the Democratic Party and, say, the Working Families Party or the Green Party, and voters can vote based on the coalition they believe in.

  • Gives voters more meaningful options
  • Encourages cooperation over polarization
  • Builds bridges between aligned movements

Expand Voting Access: Early Voting, Online Registration, and Same-Day Sign-Up

Texas consistently ranks near the bottom in voter turnout — not because people don’t care, but because the system puts up barriers. We fix that by making voting more convenient, accessible, and fair:

  • Guarantee at least 14 days of early voting statewide
  • Require weekend voting, including at least one full Sunday and one extended 12-hour weekday
  • Expand polling locations in rural, suburban, and working-class areas to shorten lines and reduce wait times
  • Tie the number of polling places and mail drop boxes to population size, so no community is left behind
  • Allow full online voter registration for all eligible Texans
  • Enable same-day voter registration so no one is turned away just for missing a deadline

Voting should be safe, simple, and secure.

End Gerrymandering: Fair Maps by Independent Experts

Texas is one of the most gerrymandered states in the country — where politicians pick their voters instead of earning their support. That ends now.

  • Require an Independent Citizens Redistricting Commission to redraw legislative and congressional maps after every Census
  • Maps must follow strict nonpartisan criteria: no favoritism by race, party, or incumbent protection
  • End map manipulation that dilutes the voices of communities of color, working-class Texans, or political minorities
  • Begin immediate redistricting under new rules — not in 2030, but now

Let’s return power to the voters, where it belongs. Fair maps. Fair elections. A fair shot for all Texans.

Texas Care Plan

Expand Medicaid. Cut Costs. Save Lives.

  • Finally accept federal funds for Medicaid expansion, cover over 1 million uninsured Texans
  • Create a private option (like Arkansas and Montana) to use federal funds for a Texas solution
  • Use state oversight to keep costs low and access wide
“You should be able to see a doctor without skipping rent. We’ve already paid for this — it’s time we use it.”

Would "Texas Care" Cost the State Basically Nothing?

Short answer: Pretty close to nothing.
Long answer: Let’s break it down.

Cost Structure of Medicaid Expansion

  • Federal Match: The federal government covers 90% of the cost for newly eligible adults. Texas pays just 10%, and even less if we expand soon, thanks to a +5% bonus for all Medicaid spending (for 2 years).
  • Estimated Cost to Texas: Urban Institute estimates Texas would spend $1–2 billion per year on its 10% share. But Texas already spends more than that on uncompensated care, ER visits, and county hospital district reimbursements.
  • Net Effect? Studies (including from the Texas Hospital Association) suggest Texas would break even or even save money: $100–300 million in annual net savings depending on rollout strategy. Bonus: Reduces hospital closures, especially in rural areas.

Healthcare Access for All Texans

  • Protect Rural Hospitals: Ensure rural hospitals have funding to stay open, reduce staff turnover, and meet community needs.
  • Expand VA Clinic Assistance: Establish a dedicated Veteran Health Fund from the state surplus to improve staffing and resources at VA clinics.
  • Mental Health Services: Expand community-based mental health care and integrate mental health services into all primary care settings.

Education and Public Schools

Classrooms should be safe, welcoming spaces where every student can learn and grow—free from political battles. The Texas Legislature must stop attacking public education and instead support teachers and students.

  • Fully Fund Every Student: Tie the Public Education Basic Allotment to inflation moving forward so schools get the funding they need, year after year, without politics getting in the way.
  • End Political Overreach in the Classroom: Empower teachers and parents to lead education decisions, reducing state micromanagement. This will help retain teachers and protect funding for students.
  • End Corporate Influence on Curriculum: Ban curriculum vendors from donating to legislators, SBOE members, or PACs. Require full transparency on all curriculum contracts, audits, and political donations.
  • Bridge the Digital Divide: Close gaps in broadband, devices, and tech support across Texas schools. Support 1:1 device programs where appropriate and universal school WiFi.

Personal Freedom and Core Values

"I don't think the Capitol should be telling you who to spend your life with, what to read, or what healthcare to get."

Too much time and taxpayer money are wasted on hot-button social issues that distract from the real work of government. I believe in protecting personal freedom and keeping the government out of decisions that belong to individuals and families. Texans should be free to live their lives without political interference. Respecting personal freedoms doesn’t mean ignoring shared responsibilities. We need policies that lift all Texans.

  • Protecting Personal Freedom: The government should not overstep its bounds.
  • Book Bans and Censorship: Families, not politicians, should decide what’s appropriate in their homes.
  • Gun Ownership: This is a personal choice, and that right should be respected with common sense regulations.
  • Abortion Access and Healthcare: These are decisions to be made between you and your doctor, not the government.

My campaign avoids the distractions of culture war politics. I’m focused on real issues: investing in public schools, protecting Medicaid and healthcare access, and advocating for those who are too often left out or pushed aside. These aren’t just political positions; they reflect how I was raised. I believe in helping people, no matter where they come from, and leaving your community better than you found it. That’s what public service should be about.
