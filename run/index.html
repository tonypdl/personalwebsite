Tony Ponce de Leon for Texas House District 132

About Tony

Tony Ponce de Leon is an educator and community activist running to represent Texas State House District 132.

Platform

Affordability for Working Texans
  • Cut the State Sales Tax

    The Plan: Lower the state sales tax from 6.25% to 5% to put more money in Texans' pockets.
    How It Happens: Use part of the state's $24B surplus; replace revenue over time with marijuana/gambling taxes and corporate loophole closures.
    Impact: Saves families $300–$400/year; reduces regressive tax burden; builds trust in public investment.

  • End Sales Tax on Essentials

    The Plan: Make essentials tax-free: groceries, hygiene items, school supplies, and Texas-made goods.
    How It Happens: Expand exemption list through legislation; create a "Certified Texas Product" program.
    Impact: Reduces cost of living; supports local businesses; adds fairness to the tax code.

  • Expand Sales Tax Holidays

    The Plan: Add three week-long holidays: August (back-to-school), January (winter essentials), April (storm prep).
    How It Happens: Extend and broaden existing tax holiday structure; align with local governments.
    Impact: Predictable savings for families; boosts retail; promotes preparedness.

  • Property Tax Relief for Those Who Serve

    The Plan: Carve-outs for teachers, nurses, EMTs, veterans, disabled Texans, correctional officers.
    How It Happens: State-funded exemptions based on service and income level; hold districts harmless.
    Impact: Helps essential workers stay housed; redistributes relief more equitably than blanket exemptions.

A Government That Works for Us
  • Campaign Finance Reform

    The Plan: End corporate PAC dominance and pay-to-play politics.
    How It Happens: Ban corporate PAC donations; real-time disclosure; limit lobbying loopholes.
    Impact: Fairer elections; levels the field for grassroots candidates.

  • People Over MegaCorporations

    The Plan: End tax favoritism for luxury developers and billion-dollar corporations.
    How It Happens: Close "dark store" and franchise loopholes; luxury property surtax; land-use fees.
    Impact: Billions in new revenue; shifts burden from working families to those who can afford more.

  • Sunset Special Tax Breaks

    The Plan: Require regular review of all corporate tax exemptions.
    How It Happens: Automatic 5- or 10-year sunsets; Public Benefit Review Board.
    Impact: Ends permanent handouts; redirects billions to schools, infrastructure, and relief.

  • Open Up Democracy

    The Plan: Expand ballot access and voting rights.
    How It Happens: Legalize fusion voting; reduce third-party petition requirements; use dues-paying/convention attendance as access metrics; expand early voting.
    Impact: More choice for voters; real competition; more participation from young, independent, and new voters.

  • Progressive Property Tax Reform

    The Plan: Tax the top end of the market to fund shared needs.
    How It Happens: Luxury surtax on properties over $2M; tiered second-home assessments.
    Impact: Funds tax relief and public investment without raising rates on everyday Texans.

Smart Revenue, Not Higher Taxes
  • Legalize & Tax Marijuana and Gambling

    The Plan: Regulate both markets and use revenue for core priorities.
    How It Happens: Legislation + licensing structure; 15–20% tax rate; destination casino model; local opt-in.
    Impact: $1–1.3B/year in new revenue; job creation; cuts into black market.

  • Luxury & Corporate Surtaxes

    The Plan: Target ultra-luxury goods and ultra-wealthy firms.
    How It Happens: Surtaxes on jets, yachts, $2M+ homes, mega-profits, and stock buyback firms.
    Impact: Hundreds of millions in new funding; lets working Texans pay less.

  • Fix the Franchise Tax

    The Plan: Modernize Texas’ margins tax to ensure fairness.
    How It Happens: Graduated rates; minimum tax floor; close passive entity loopholes.
    Impact: $500–700M/year in new revenue; protects small biz, ends big-corp freeloading.

Invest in Texas' Future
  • Fully Fund Public Schools

    The Plan: Raise per-student allotment and teacher pay.
    How It Happens: Raise the Basic Allotment by $1,000–1,500; COLA indexing; teacher pay floor; end vouchers.
    Impact: Better learning environments; improved retention; stronger public education system.

  • Modernize ERCOT with Clean Energy

    The Plan: Build a green, reliable grid that won’t fail Texans.
    How It Happens: Invest in battery storage, microgrids, and interconnection; Green Grid Fund.
    Impact: Fewer blackouts; more jobs; energy independence and innovation.

  • Expand Rural Broadband & Water Infrastructure

    The Plan: Bring high-speed internet and clean water to all Texans.
    How It Happens: Use state + federal grants, sin tax revenue; partner with co-ops and local utilities.
    Impact: Equitable digital access; improved public health; economic growth in underserved areas.

  • Mental Health Care for All, Especially Veterans

    The Plan: Expand access to clinics, crisis response, and VA coordination.
    How It Happens: Fund mobile teams, local clinics, school-based support; satellite VA facilities.
    Impact: Reduces ER strain, suicide, and homelessness; helps veterans access care close to home.
